## Research Sources
- AfterQuery (lapa.ninja/ai): Academic CMU Serif + terracotta on lavender-white — research legitimacy aesthetic. Warm brown as primary text colour (rgb(76,49,12) for Ape AI, similar warmth applied here)
- Stripe Sessions (godly.website): Ultra-light Söhne (weight 250) at 89px on warm parchment rgb(249,247,247). Deep purple text. Editorial magazine quality
- Tavus (lapa.ninja/ai): "Perfectlynineties" retro serif on warm parchment rgb(247,244,239). Warm editorial brand warmth through unusual typeface choice
- Godly meta-trend: Serif revival in technical products (IBM Plex Serif for Railway, Domaine for Resend, Gilda Display for World Labs) — serifs signal authority and editorial refinement

## 3 Key Decisions
1. **Warm brown text (#2A1A0E) not black**: Inspired by Ape AI's rgb(76,49,12) primary text. Signals warmth and approachability vs cold digital precision — appropriate for an academic tool.
2. **Georgia serif for paper titles and synthesis blocks**: Simulates the academic paper reading experience. Users mentally shift to "reading mode" when typography matches the format they expect from papers.
3. **Colour-coded synthesis sections**: Indigo sidebar = introduction, sage green = findings, terracotta = gaps. Each synthesis section type gets a distinct left-border signal — reduces cognitive load when scanning a generated literature review.

## Screens
1. Search — hero with large serif headline, recent searches, trending topics

3. Paper Detail — stats row, AI summary block, related papers, key concepts tags
